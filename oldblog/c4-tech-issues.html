News

In case you didn't know, there's quite a gap between the amount of women programmers vs men programmers. Heck, I got a scholarship just for being a woman. Well, I'm glad a lot of companies are beginning to focus more on this issue and some recent events have also increased the awareness of the question on equal pay. Women in the tech industry are consistently dominated by the male population (also got to point out, we get paid LESS for the same position compared to males), to the point where asking for a raise or determining how much you're worth (do you know how much you should be paid when you get outta here? And how much you should be getting paid after a couple years of experience?) becomes a lot more challenging and less fair than it should be. Of course it's not just in this industry that women face this dilemma, but it is apparent the male:female ratio is so large that this issue needs to be addressed.

In this article you can find the video to the interview where Satya Nadella, CEO of Microsoft, provided some an ill-advised comment about women and whether or not they should be asking for raises. The reaction to the video, followed by his public apology shows that this issue is receiving notice, but just barely. And just a few weeks ago, Emma Watson delivered a major speech on gender equality and how women shouldn't be the only ones standing up for themselves, but that men should join in on this cause. And that's how we can slowly make our transition as a society and in the tech industry to show that women are worth just as much as men and we can bring just as much to the tech world.
