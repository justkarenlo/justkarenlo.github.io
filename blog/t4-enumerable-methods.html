"Know-How"

After doing some research on .group_by,.cycle, and .map, I decided to focus on .group_by (because this was brand new to me and also...happened to think of a way to incorporate Friends). So here goes:

.group_by can take a block of multiple elements, such as an array or range, and turn them into a hash based on the condition you place. Say I wanted to group the Friends characters' names by the length of the name...here's how I would use the .group_by method to accomplish this:

Input: First you'll have the object you want your method to act on (group of names in this instance), your .group_by method, and then your conditional block.

Output: keys that correspond to the length of the name and values, which are the names that have that length.

  ["Ross","Rachel","Phoebe","Joey","Monica","Chandler"].group_by {|name| name.length}

  => {4=>["Ross", "Joey"], 6=>["Rachel", "Phoebe", "Monica"], 8=>["Chandler"]}

GREAT! You have just written a function that allows you to know which Friends share the same name length. Don't know why exactly you would want to do that, but you CAN. And being able to do something even when you don't need to is such a great freedom, ain't it? Now you know how to group_by right? Obviously you can use this method for other types of problems, play with it, manipulate it, break it..that's how we learn! I hope this post helped!
